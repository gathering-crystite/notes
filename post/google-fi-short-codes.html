Google Fi Short Codes

Posted on 2018, Jan 27 One min read

During a suport call with Google FI for a Pixel 2, I was told about some short codes. The codes can be used to obtain your current network information, repair a bad activation, turn on the Auto Swich feature, or connect to specific carriers.

On the call, I used both of these codes:

*#*#344636#*#* and *#*#34777#*#*

After a little further ressearch, these are all the codes I found more of these online.

Force connection to Sprint:  *#*#34777#*#*
Force connection to T-Mobile:  *#*#34866#*#*
Force connection to next carrier: *#*#346398#*#*
Turn on auto switch: *#*#342886#*#*
Repair bad activation: *#*#34963#*#*
Obtain current network information: *#*#344636#*#*

To use these codes, simply dial the number on your default phone app. You don’t need to press the button to dial the number. During my research, I found this site helpful:

https://forum.xda-developers.com/nexus-6/general/project-fi-fi-dialer-codes-to-force-t3132393

However, I was calling about a Pixel 2, and the link above has “nexus-6” in it. Some of these codes may be old. Use these at your own risk.
